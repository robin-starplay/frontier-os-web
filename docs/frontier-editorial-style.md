# Frontier Editorial Style

Frontier writes for investors and operators. Copy should be clear enough to scan quickly and precise enough to support a decision.

## Voice

Write with restraint. Use direct language, investor terminology and operational language. State the decision, evidence, limitation or action without promotional framing.

Prefer short sentences. Use full stops to separate ideas. Use commas where the relationship is simple.

## Do not use

- Em dashes
- En dashes as sentence punctuation
- Repeated or excessive semicolons
- Artificially symmetrical lists written for rhythm rather than meaning
- Repeated “not X, but Y” constructions
- Seamlessly
- Leverage, except when referring to financial leverage
- Revolutionary
- Cutting-edge
- Powerful
- Actionable insights
- At a glance
- Delve
- Furthermore
- Moreover
- Holistic
- Streamline
- Unlock
- Comprehensive
- Robust
- Designed to
- “Helps you” or “helps us”

En dashes remain acceptable for numeric and date ranges. Use “Not stated”, “Not available” or “Not assessed” for missing values.

## Preferred construction

- Lead with the conclusion.
- Use one idea per sentence.
- Name the evidence or limitation.
- Use concrete nouns and active verbs.
- End workflows with a decision, owner or next action.
- Use sentence case.
- Remove adjectives that do not change the meaning.

## Examples

Avoid:

> Frontier seamlessly turns powerful data into better decisions.

Prefer:

> Frontier structures company and deal evidence into a reviewable investment view.

Avoid:

> The result is not just analysis, but a complete decision framework.

Prefer:

> The result records the current view, supporting evidence and next action.

## Review test

Before shipping copy, ask:

1. Can the sentence be shorter?
2. Does every adjective add information?
3. Is the subject clear?
4. Does the sentence state evidence, uncertainty or action precisely?
5. Would an investment professional use this wording in a working session?

The target is the compression and clarity of financial reporting, while retaining Frontier’s own vocabulary and voice.
